  <!-- Carte : What is a determiner? -->
  <div class="card">
    <h2>🏷️ What is a determiner?</h2>
    <div class="definition-mg">
      🔹 A <strong>determiner</strong> is a small word that comes before a noun to clarify it.<br><br>
      🔹 It gives information about <strong>definiteness</strong> (specific or general), <strong>quantity</strong>, <strong>possession</strong>, or <strong>demonstration</strong>.<br><br>
      🔹 In English, determiners are essential: they introduce nouns and help us understand exactly what we are talking about.<br><br>
      <span class="icone">📌</span> <strong>Main types for beginners:</strong><br><br>
      <span class="icone">📌</span> <strong>Articles</strong> (definite, indefinite)<br><br>
      <span class="icone">📌</span> <strong>Possessive determiners</strong> (my, your, his...)<br><br>
      <span class="icone">📌</span> <strong>Demonstrative determiners</strong> (this, that, these, those)<br><br>
      <span class="icone">📌</span> <strong>Quantifiers</strong> (some, any, much, many...)
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- 1. Articles -->
  <div class="card">
    <h2>1️⃣ Articles (definite & indefinite)</h2>
    <div class="definition-mg">
      <strong>📖 Explanation:</strong><br>
      <strong>Definite article</strong> → <strong>the</strong> (specific, known).<br>
      <strong>Indefinite articles</strong> → <strong>a</strong> / <strong>an</strong> (general, not specific).<br>
      Use <strong>a</strong> before consonant sounds, <strong>an</strong> before vowel sounds.
    </div>
    <div class="conjugaison-ligne">
      <strong>Forms:</strong><br>
      <strong>The</strong> → singular & plural, all genders<br>
      <strong>A</strong> → singular, consonant sound<br>
      <strong>An</strong> → singular, vowel sound
    </div>
    <div class="exemple-modern">
      <div class="exo-titre">
        <span>✨</span> Examples
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">The cat is on the roof. (specific cat)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">I saw a dog in the park. (one dog, not specific)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">She ate an apple. (apple → vowel sound)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">The children are playing. (specific children)</span>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="highlight">
      💡 <strong>Remember:</strong> Use "a" before consonant sounds (a book, a house) and "an" before vowel sounds (an hour, an orange).
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- 2. Possessive determiners -->
  <div class="card">
    <h2>2️⃣ Possessive determiners (my, your, his...)</h2>
    <div class="definition-mg">
      <strong>📖 Explanation:</strong><br>
      Possessive determiners show <strong>ownership</strong> or <strong>belonging</strong>. They agree with the <strong>owner</strong>, not the object.
    </div>
    <div class="conjugaison-ligne">
      <strong>Forms:</strong><br>
      <strong>My</strong> → 1st person singular (I)<br>
      <strong>Your</strong> → 2nd person singular (you)<br>
      <strong>His / Her / Its</strong> → 3rd person singular (he, she, it)<br>
      <strong>Our</strong> → 1st person plural (we)<br>
      <strong>Your</strong> → 2nd person plural (you all)<br>
      <strong>Their</strong> → 3rd person plural (they)
    </div>
    <div class="exemple-modern">
      <div class="exo-titre">
        <span>✨</span> Examples
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">This is my book.</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">Is that your pen?</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">His sister is a doctor.</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">Her name is Emma.</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">Our house is big.</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">Their car is red.</span>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="highlight">
      💡 <strong>Note:</strong> "Its" (no apostrophe) is the possessive of "it". "It's" means "it is".
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- 3. Demonstrative determiners -->
  <div class="card">
    <h2>3️⃣ Demonstrative determiners (this, that, these, those)</h2>
    <div class="definition-mg">
      <strong>📖 Explanation:</strong><br>
      Demonstratives point to <strong>specific</strong> things. They show <strong>distance</strong> (near / far) and <strong>number</strong> (singular / plural).
    </div>
    <div class="conjugaison-ligne">
      <strong>Forms:</strong><br>
      <strong>This</strong> → singular, near (here)<br>
      <strong>That</strong> → singular, far (there)<br>
      <strong>These</strong> → plural, near (here)<br>
      <strong>Those</strong> → plural, far (there)
    </div>
    <div class="exemple-modern">
      <div class="exo-titre">
        <span>✨</span> Examples
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">This book is interesting. (here, singular)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">That car is expensive. (there, singular)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">These apples are fresh. (here, plural)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">Those houses are old. (there, plural)</span>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="highlight">
      💡 <strong>Tip:</strong> Use "this/these" for things close to you, "that/those" for things farther away.
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- 4. Quantifiers (some, any, much, many, etc.) -->
  <div class="card">
    <h2>4️⃣ Quantifiers (some, any, much, many, a lot of)</h2>
    <div class="definition-mg">
      <strong>📖 Explanation:</strong><br>
      Quantifiers express <strong>quantity</strong> or <strong>amount</strong>. They are used with countable and uncountable nouns.
    </div>
    <div class="conjugaison-ligne">
      <strong>Common quantifiers:</strong><br>
      <strong>Some</strong> → positive sentences, offers, requests (countable/uncountable)<br>
      <strong>Any</strong> → negative sentences, questions (countable/uncountable)<br>
      <strong>Much</strong> → uncountable nouns (much water)<br>
      <strong>Many</strong> → countable nouns (many books)<br>
      <strong>A lot of</strong> → both countable & uncountable (a lot of time / people)<br>
      <strong>A few</strong> → countable, small number<br>
      <strong>A little</strong> → uncountable, small amount
    </div>
    <div class="exemple-modern">
      <div class="exo-titre">
        <span>✨</span> Examples
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">I have some money. (uncountable, positive)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">Do you have any questions? (countable, question)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">There isn't much milk left. (uncountable, negative)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">She has many friends. (countable, positive)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">We need a lot of sugar. (uncountable)</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">I have a few ideas. (countable, small number)</span>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="highlight">
      💡 <strong>Remember:</strong> Use "much" with uncountable nouns (milk, water), "many" with countable nouns (books, cars).
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- 5. Summary table -->
  <div class="card">
    <h2>📌 Quick summary – Determiners</h2>
    <div class="definition-mg">
      ✅ <strong>Articles</strong> : a / an / the → specific or general<br><br>
      ✅ <strong>Possessives</strong> : my, your, his, her, its, our, their → ownership<br><br>
      ✅ <strong>Demonstratives</strong> : this, that, these, those → pointing / distance<br><br>
      ✅ <strong>Quantifiers</strong> : some, any, much, many, a lot of, etc. → quantity<br><br>
      ✅ <strong>Numbers</strong> : one, two, three... (also determiners)<br><br>
      👉 <strong>Key idea</strong> : a determiner always comes before a noun and helps define it.
    </div>
    <div class="exemple-modern" style="margin-top: 12px;">
      <div class="exo-titre">
        <span>🧪</span> Mixed examples
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">This is my favourite book.</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">I have some apples and a few oranges.</span>
      </div>
      <div class="exo-ligne">
        <span class="fr">Those children are our neighbours.</span>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <!-- Tips card -->
  <div class="card">
    <h2>🎯 Tips for beginners</h2>
    <div class="definition-mg" style="margin-bottom: 6px;">
      <ul class="liste-avec-lignes-vides">
        <li>Always use a determiner before a singular countable noun (a dog, the car).</li>
        <li>Plural countable nouns can stand alone (dogs are friendly) or with determiners (those dogs).</li>
        <li>Uncountable nouns often use quantifiers (some water, much rice).</li>
        <li>Practice with "a" vs "an": listen to the first sound (a university, an umbrella).</li>
        <li>Possessives agree with the owner, not the object (her book, his book).</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <div class="highlight" style="margin-top: 8px;">
      🌱 <strong>Remember:</strong> Determiners are everywhere in English. Learn them step by step, and don't be afraid to make mistakes!
    </div>
  </div>
